    Plant name: Fiddle leaf fig

     

    Introduction:

    A popular indoor plant, native to Africa. They thrive in warm and wet conditions. Every two weeks remember to dust the leaves with damp cloth. They require bright, filtered light to look their best. Direct sunlight might burn the leaves. Needs moderate amounts of water in the soil. Temperatures from 15 - 26 degrees Celsius.

     

    Plant name: Rubber plant

     

    Introduction:

    Best to get a young rubber plant and it will adapt to indoor conditions. Can grow very tall within a few years. It grows best in summers. Well draining soil is most important as rubber plants do not like to sit in wet soil. Balance in water is crucial. During the dormant month0 you may only need to water it once or twice a month. Needs a lot of light. Best to avoid direct light.

     

    Plant name: Areca Palm

     

    Introduction:

    Areca Palm is an easy-to-grow and low maintenance houseplant. Areca Palm prefers bright, indirect light to part shade. It can tolerate low light conditions, and too much light can scorch the leaves. Place it in a well-lit room because insufficient light will result in slow growth.

     

    Plant name: Dracaena

     

    Introduction:

    The dracaena plant is a popular ornamental houseplant, grown both indoors and outdoors in subtropical climates. It reaches a height of about three feet indoors and has a bushy tree type of look. Its glossy leaves can grow up to one foot long and a couple of inches wide.

     

    Plant name: Snake plant

     

    Introduction:

    Snake plants need little water and light to survive. Can grow anywhere from 12 inches to 3 feet tall. Can be watered once every two weeks or once every month during winters. Do not over water snake plants. Let the soil be dry between watering. Ensure plenty of sunlight.
