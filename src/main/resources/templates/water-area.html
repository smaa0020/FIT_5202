    Urban Water Space in Melbourne

    Rivers, creeks and wetlands are central to everyday life, sustaining a complex ecosystem of plants and animals – and providing natural spaces for people to enjoy. The plants and animals that rely on rivers live together in a delicate balance. However, this can be upset by human activities that change a river’s natural state, sometimes with widespread and destructive results.Nowadays, the City of Melbourne have one constructed wetland system, located in Royal Park and the lowest section of of Yarra River.

     

    Tips to protect the rivers in urban area:


    For the Commercial & Industrial Areas

    1. The waterway, river banks, riverside parkland and recreational trails are not overshadowed.

    2. Retention of the natural topography of the riverbanks.

    3. Elements of identified industrial heritage are retained.

    4. New buildings do not overwhelm the width of the waterway.

    5. Further encroachment of the built form into the waterways corridor is avoided.

    6. Strong landscaped edge to river and adjoining open space is to screen views to buildings is maintained and strengthened.

    7. Tree canopy is retained and enhanced as the dominant visual elements along the skyline.

    8. Wastewater and stormwater are managed to avoid waterway pollution.


    For the Residential Areas

    1. Low density character is maintained.

    2. Visibility of buildings from the river, adjoining parkland and the opposite bank is minimised or avoided where possible.

    3. Strong landscaped edge to river and open spaces to screen views to buildings.

    4. Further encroachment of built form into the river corridor is avoided.

    5. Tree canopy is retained and enhanced as dominant visual element
