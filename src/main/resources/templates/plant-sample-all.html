    Plant name: Fern

     

    Introduction:

    Ferns are a popular indoor plant. Ferns in hanging pots/tubs are a very attractive option for your office. Growing conditions differ for different ferns. Ferns do not need intense sunlight. Ideally indoor ferns will need some humidity and some indirect light. Suitable temperatures are 15-21 degrees Celsius.

     

    Plant name: Ivy

     

    Introduction:

    Ivy is a very popular indoor vine. It is suitable for many lighting conditions. It is a very low maintenance plant. Sufficient drainage is required and avoid using a big pot as it will keep the soil moist. Indirect sunlight is recommended. Ivy are known to tolerate moist and dry soil. This plant will be best for cooler temperatures- 10 to 24 degree celsius.

     

    Plant name: Supertunia Petunias

     

    Introduction:

    Petunia 'Supertunia Bordeaux' is a mounding trailing annual or perennial boasting masses of soft plummy-pink flowers adorned with rich plum-purple veins from late spring to the first frosts. Extremely easy to grow, Petunias thrive in full sun to part shade in moderately fertile, humus-rich, moist, well-drained soil.

     

    Plant name: Impatiens

     

    Introduction:

    Impatiens plants are one of the most popular annual flowers, due to their brightly colored blooms and their ability to grow in shady areas. Although technically tropical perennials, these plants are grown as annuals in all but the warmest regions (zones 10 to 12).

     

    Plant name: Trailing Pansies

     

    Introduction:

    These trailing plants grow 6 to 8 inches tall and spread 15 to 18 inches. Like Cool Waves, they're hardy to zone 5 and prefer full sun. For a wide range of colors, check out the Wonder Fall pansy series. Their large blooms come in shades of blue, lavender, rose and purple.

     

    Plant name: Fiddle leaf fig

     

    Introduction:

    A popular indoor plant, native to Africa. They thrive in warm and wet conditions. Every two weeks remember to dust the leaves with damp cloth. They require bright, filtered light to look their best. Direct sunlight might burn the leaves. Needs moderate amounts of water in the soil. Temperatures from 15 - 26 degrees Celsius.

     

    Plant name: Rubber plant

     

    Introduction:

    Best to get a young rubber plant and it will adapt to indoor conditions. Can grow very tall within a few years. It grows best in summers. Well draining soil is most important as rubber plants do not like to sit in wet soil. Balance in water is crucial. During the dormant month0 you may only need to water it once or twice a month. Needs a lot of light. Best to avoid direct light.

     

    Plant name: Areca Palm

     

    Introduction:

    Areca Palm is an easy-to-grow and low maintenance houseplant. Areca Palm prefers bright, indirect light to part shade. It can tolerate low light conditions, and too much light can scorch the leaves. Place it in a well-lit room because insufficient light will result in slow growth.

     

    Plant name: Dracaena

     

    Introduction:

    The dracaena plant is a popular ornamental houseplant, grown both indoors and outdoors in subtropical climates. It reaches a height of about three feet indoors and has a bushy tree type of look. Its glossy leaves can grow up to one foot long and a couple of inches wide.

     

    Plant name: Snake plant

     

    Introduction:

    Snake plants need little water and light to survive. Can grow anywhere from 12 inches to 3 feet tall. Can be watered once every two weeks or once every month during winters. Do not over water snake plants. Let the soil be dry between watering. Ensure plenty of sunlight.

     

    Plant name: Ilex crenata

     

    Introduction:

    Ilex crenata is grown as an ornamental plant for its dense evergreen foliage, and is a popular plant among Bonsai enthusiasts. It is superficially similar in appearance to boxwood (box), and is often used in similar situations, such as low hedging; but it can readily be distinguished from boxwood by its alternate, not opposite, leaf arrangement.

     

    Plant name: Hydrangea

     

    Introduction:

    Hydrangea flowers are produced from early spring to late autumn; they grow in flowerheads (corymbs or panicles) most often at the ends of the stems. Typically the flowerheads contain two types of flowers: small non-showy flowers in the center or interior of the flowerhead, and large, showy flowers with large colorful sepals (tepals).

     

    Plant name: Dwarf conifers

     

    Introduction:

    Conifers are trees or shrubs that bear cones. Simply stated, they are conifers that do not reach the normal height for their species. True dwarf conifers average about one-twentieth the typical size, with most species growing from 1 to 6 inches annually and reaching less than 6 feet after ten years.

     

    Plant name: Sedges

     

    Introduction:

    At first glance, one might assume sedges are grasses. They are not and are held in the genus Carex. Sedges span both native varieties and hybridized versions. They are useful in moist areas, such as around ponds, but there are also types of sedge that thrive in dry regions.

     

    Plant name: Mosses

     

    Introduction:

    Mosses are non-vascular plants in the land plant division Bryophyta. They are small (a few centimetres tall) herbaceous (non-woody) plants that absorb water and nutrients mainly through their leaves and harvest carbon dioxide and sunlight to create food by photosynthesis.

     

    Plant name: Boston Fern

     

    Introduction:

    Ferns are a popular indoor plant. Boston ferns need a cool place with high humidity and indirect light. Might need additional humidity in the winters. Leaves will turn yellow if the humidity is not high enough. Ideally indoor ferns will need some humidity and some indirect light. Suitable temperatures are 15-21 degrees Celsius. They do not need too much fertiliser.

     

    Plant name: Lipstick plant

     

    Introduction:

    Provided they get enough warmth, humidity, and filtered sunlight, you'll enjoy a prolific display of flowering through much of the year. Generally, though, lipstick plants flower most abundantly in the summer and fall.

     

    Plant name: Parsley

     

    Introduction:

    This is a very common herb, which can also be used for interior decoration. It not only makes people feel refreshed, but the fresh fragrance of vegetation can also relieve people’s fatigue.

     

    Plant name: Rosemary

     

    Introduction:

    The fragrant plant is a delightful scent and is sometimes used in floral arrangements. Rosemary likes its soil a bit on the dry side, so be careful not to overwater. Allowed to flourish, a rosemary plant will grow into a full-sized bush.

     

    Plant name: Bird Nest Fern

     

    Introduction:

    The Bird’s Nest Fern has large simple fronds that are tropical-like in that they resemble banana leaves. Their fronds are light green, often crinkled or wavy, and rise from a central rosette. The rosette is fuzzy where the emerging fronds resemble bird’s eggs - hence the common name.

     

    Plant name: Calla lilies

     

    Introduction:

    The calla lily (Zantedeschia sp.) is an extraordinary flower. This beautiful plant, available in a multitude of colors, grows from rhizomes and is ideal for use in beds and borders. You can also grow calla lilies in containers, either outdoors or in a sunny window as houseplants.

     

    Plant name: Duckweed

     

    Introduction:

    Duckweeds are tiny, free-floating, aquatic green plants commonly found in lentic or slowly moving water bodies. Members of the family Lemnaceae include the world's smallest angiosperms and are commonly known as duckweeds.

     

    Plant name: Arrowhead plants

     

    Introduction:

    Arrowhead Plant, is derived from the spade-like shape of its leaves. Fast growing and lush, the Arrowhead is constantly unfurling new foliage, in varieties ranging from dark green with strong white variegation to hues of lime and even pink

     

    Plant name: Water lettuce

     

    Introduction:

    Water lettuce is often used in tropical aquariums to provide cover for fry and small fish. It is also helpful as it outcompetes algae for nutrients in the water, thereby preventing massive algal blooms.

     

    Plant name: Wax plant

     

    Introduction:

    Hoya plants, native to Southern India and found throughout Eastern Asia, are often called Wax Plants due to their foliage and flowers having a wax-like feel to them. Given enough light, the Hoya plant will produce lovely delicate flowers.

     

    Plant name: Burro's Tail

     

    Introduction:

    Easy to maintain plant. Let the soil dry out before watering. This is native to Mexico and each stem can grow upto 3 feet.

     

    Plant name: Hydrangea

     

    Introduction:

    Hydrangea flowers are produced from early spring to late autumn; they grow in flowerheads (corymbs or panicles) most often at the ends of the stems. Typically the flowerheads contain two types of flowers: small non-showy flowers in the center or interior of the flowerhead, and large, showy flowers with large colorful sepals (tepals).

     

    Plant name: Angel Wings Cactus

     

    Introduction:

    Bunny ears cactus plant, also called angel's wings, has ease of care combined with an original appearance. The thick pads of this plant are adorned with fuzzy glochids, or short bristles, that resemble the fur of rabbits and grow in earlike pairs.

     

    Plant name: Old Lady Cactus

     

    Introduction:

    Mammillaria hahniana, also known by its common name 'Old Lady Cactus', is a popular powder puff type cactus that is sun-loving and easy to grow. The plant, which belongs to the Cactaceae family, is particularly attractive with its lone spherical stems and flowers in hues of red-purple.

     

    Plant name: Echeveria Hyalina

     

    Introduction:

    Echeveria hyalina is a pale beauty. Narrow, fleshy leaves bluish-white. Cooler temperatures bring on pale pink highlights.
