    • Novice Level

    If you want to bring some nature to Melbourne CBD by create a garden by yourself, it is difficult to know how to start when you are not familiar with gardening. Even the most experienced gardener needs to have the right tools. Thus, the list of necessary gardening tools we provide will take you on the right path to plant a beautiful garden at the beginning.

    Hand Trowel

    The trowel is a small tool, essential for planting, transplanting and potting. Look for a handle with a firm connection between the blade and the handle, and hold it comfortably on the handle, avoid hand fatigue and provide you with the best control

     

     

     

    Orchard Shears

    Garden pruning machines, also called pruning shears, pruning shears or clippers, are very useful hand tools around the garden. You will need it to trim and shape plants and shrubs, as well as to remove dead skin. High-quality pruning shears will last a long time, stay sharper for a longer time, be able to beat thick branches, and be more comfortable to use.

     

     

     

    Garden Hoe

    Gardens are essential for cultivating the soil, mowing the lawn, and performing many other gardening tasks. There are many varieties of this versatile gardening tool, but the conventional Dutch or flat head can meet the needs of most gardeners. You will find it very useful when preparing beds for planting and weeding

     

     

     

    Gardening Gloves

    Thorns and debris can be annoying and uncomfortable if you touch the plant directly with your hands, because a good pair of gardening gloves can prevent them from penetrating. Gardening tasks can wreak havoc on you and make them feel pain and annoyance. Fortunately, there are a variety of gloves that can provide better protection and comfort than ever before.

     

     

     

    Watering Can

    The healthy growth of plants is inseparable from sunlight and water. Watering cans are also one of the indispensable tools in gardening design. You need to water the plants on time according to the needs of different species to ensure that they can draw enough water to grow.

     

     

     

    Garden Fork

    The garden fork is one of the most diligent tools in any gardener's arsenal and is a multifunctional device. It can be used to aerate the lawn, break up compacted soil, inject air into the soil, mix in soil treatment, remove stones and weeds, and soften hard ground. Its sharp and strong tines penetrate difficult terrain more easily than a shovel.

     

     

     

    Shovel

    A shovel is a prerequisite for most work around the garden, especially including digging and removing or adding loose gardening materials (such as compost, fertilizer, mulch, soil, etc.). Whether you are removing weeds or spreading mulch, digging deep holes or composting, high-quality shovel is very valuable to you
